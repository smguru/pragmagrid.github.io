Call for Demos 

On behalf of PRAGMA 31 Program Committee, we are soliciting demos for the PRAGMA31 workshop in Bangkok, Thailand, hosted by Thammasat University. All PRAGMA 31 attendees are encouraged to submit abstracts to the workshop.

 

 The abstract should be between 300 and 450 words and include the following information:
1) Title of the demo
2) Names and institutions
3) A brief abstract
4) Requirements for the demo (e.g. time, network, etc)

 **Registration is required prior to abstract submission [Registration] **

Important Dates:

Submission deadline: August 24th, 2016

Author notification: August 27th, 2016

We are looking forward to your submissions. If you have any questions regarding the demo abstract submission, please contact our demo chairs. For technical questions, please contact pragma31.inquiry@gmail.com  
 
Demo Chairs
1) Jason Haga <jh.haga@aist.go.jp>
2) Kohei Ichikawa <ichikawa@is.naist.jp>
3) Renato Figueiredo <renato@acis.ufl.edu>

 

  Call for Posters

 All PRAGMA31 attendees, especially students, are welcomed to submit poster abstracts to PRAGMA 31 Workshop.  


The abstract should be less than or equal to 450 words and covering the following information: 
1) Title of the poster
2) Names and institutions
3) A brief abstract (less than or equal to 450 words)

**Registration is required prior to abstract submission [Registration] **

 

We strongly recommend all submissions use the word template provided [Poster Abstract Template]

All submissions will be reviewed by PRAGMA Students Steering Committee and PRAGMA PC members. Review feedback and acceptance notice will be issued on August 25th, 2016.

Important Dates: 

Submission deadline: August 24th, 2016 

Author notification: August 27th, 2016 

Workshop Registration deadline: August 28th, 2016 

 

 Call for Participation: PRAGMA Student Hackathon

Undergraduate and master’s degree students (currently enrolled in Computer Science, Computer Engineering and other IT-related programs) are eligible to apply. Women and underrepresented minorities are especially encouraged to apply.

To apply: Go to [Student Hackathon Registration] and register to participate.

Important Dates: 

 Registration deadline: August 20th, 2016 (Extended with a firm deadline)
Invited Applicant notification: August 25th, 2016 

Location: Department of Computer Science, Thammasat University, Rangsit Campus.

Dates and times: September 5-6, 2016 (Starts at 9am on September 5th and ends at 4pm on September 6th)

 

Proposed Activities

Topic (1) Extension of the existing PRAGMA Cloud Testbed by setting up new computing nodes at the Thammasat University site and proposal to improve the process.
(The number of teams participating in Topic 1 is limited to a total of three.)
Possible project:
● Setting up a PRAGMA-ENT node and registering the node to the PRAGMA Cloud Scheduler. Possibly start a virtual cluster via cloud scheduler
More information on Topic (1)
 
 
Topic (2) Applications that add more visibility and analytical ability to PRAGMA Cloud Testbed services
Possible projects:
● An API for querying the Cloud Scheduler database for registered sites and new real-time visualizations of the results for PRAGMA Testbed sites.
● New visualization that monitors current bookings (running virtual clusters), on what sites, and what is remaining site availability. Possibly name of a virtual cluster.
More information on Topic (2)
 
 
Topic (3) Applications that search, query, discover or generally mine biodiversity data through Lifemapper APIs
Possible projects:
● New visualizations to summarize the contents of a Lifemapper datastore (archive) by species and time.
● New visualizations to provide related information to decide if new compute nodes are needed to calculate jobs for a Lifemapper Server instance.
More information on Topic (3)
